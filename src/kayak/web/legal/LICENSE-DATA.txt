                         KAYAK DATA LICENSE FALLBACK

This file is the kayak engine's generic fallback data-license notice. A
deployment should provide its own DATASET_DIR/LICENSE-DATA.txt describing the
actual dataset, provenance, attribution, and any non-default terms. When that
dataset file exists, the build publishes it instead of this fallback.

================================================================================
DATABASE AND METADATA
================================================================================

Unless a deployment supplies a more specific dataset license, curated river
metadata is published under Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0
International (CC BY-NC 4.0).

    https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/

Curated metadata may include reach descriptions, classifications,
source-to-gauge linkings, calculation expressions for virtual gauges, rating
tables, geometry, guidebook references, and other human-maintained fields.

You are free to:
    Share   - copy and redistribute the material in any medium or format.
    Adapt   - remix, transform, and build upon the material.

Under the following terms:
    Attribution    - give appropriate credit, link to this license, and
                     indicate if changes were made.
    NonCommercial  - do not use this material for commercial purposes.

================================================================================
CALCULATED GAUGE SERIES
================================================================================

Calculated or virtual gauge series are licensed under the same terms as the
curated metadata unless the dataset's own license says otherwise. Although
their source observations may be public-domain data, the formulas are
curatorial work.

================================================================================
OBSERVATION TIME-SERIES
================================================================================

Raw observation values fetched from government agencies are generally public
domain or public-record data at the originating source. Check and credit the
originating agency when redistributing observations.
